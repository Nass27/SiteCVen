Degree in Web and Mobile Web Development (Bac +2)

AFIP formations

The curriculum for a degree in Web and Mobile Web Development provides a comprehensive education aimed at equipping students with essential skills to excel in the ever-evolving field of web design and development. Throughout this program, students gain a solid foundation in programming, becoming familiar with languages such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and other front-end web technologies. They learn to design attractive and functional user interfaces while incorporating best design practices. Back-end development is also an integral part of the program, introducing languages such as PHP and Python. Students explore database management, understand server concepts and hosting management, and learn to create robust and secure applications. Responsive design and mobile application development are also crucial aspects of the program. Students are trained in designing adaptive interfaces that provide an optimal user experience across a variety of devices, including smartphones and tablets. In parallel, web project management is often addressed to teach students the organizational skills needed to plan, develop, and successfully deploy web projects. Agile methodologies and project management tools are often integrated into the curriculum. Finally, technological monitoring and problem-solving are encouraged to enable students to stay updated with the latest industry trends and technologies. Thus, at the end of this two-year program in Web and Mobile Web Development, students are well-equipped to enter the workforce with a thorough understanding of both front-end and back-end web development principles.
